The kiwi is a small, flightless bird native exclusively to New Zealand, and serves as the country's most iconic national symbol. Unlike most birds, the kiwi is nocturnal, has nostrils at the tip of its long beak for sniffing out insects and worms, and lays eggs that are enormous relative to its body size. Sadly, kiwi populations have declined significantly due to habitat loss and predators introduced by humans, making conservation efforts across New Zealand critical to the species' survival.
