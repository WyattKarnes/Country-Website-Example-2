Milford Sound is a dramatic fiord on the southwest coast of New Zealand's South Island, carved by glaciers over thousands of years. Towering cliffs, dense rainforest, and cascading waterfalls like Stirling Falls and Lady Bowen Falls line its steep walls. It's often called the "eighth wonder of the world" and is one of New Zealand's most visited natural attractions.
